The inflection point

The category is being redefined, and Fireflies is positioned to write the new definition.

The AI meeting assistant market hit $1.6B in 2024. It's expected to reach $6.2B by 2033 at a 21% CAGR. Otter.ai, the category pioneer, is now facing a federal lawsuit over consent. Microsoft Copilot and Google Gemini are adding meeting intelligence as bundled features. The window for a standalone, cross-platform meeting intelligence layer to become indispensable is open. It won't stay open forever.

The real competitive threat

It's not Fathom or Otter. It's meeting intelligence becoming a checkbox feature.

Zoom, Teams, and Google Meet are all adding AI summaries natively. The risk isn't another notetaker. It's the meeting platforms themselves absorbing basic transcription and making standalone tools look redundant. Fireflies' answer to this is correct: go beyond the meeting platforms by working across all of them, offering depth they can't match, and owning the layer that actually activates institutional knowledge. The messaging needs to say this more directly.

What Talk to Fireflies actually signals

The first meeting assistant to bring the world's knowledge into the room.

The Perplexity integration isn't a feature. It's a category statement. No other meeting tool has done this. When a prospect can say "Hey Fireflies, compare our pricing to Competitor X" mid-call and get a live answer, the product stops being a notetaker and becomes a decision support layer. That's a fundamentally different story to tell.

The 200+ AI apps moment

From "summarize & store" to "summarize & do."

The April 2025 launch of 200+ agentic AI apps shifted Fireflies' value prop fundamentally. Sales can auto-update CRM. Recruiting can generate scorecards. Ops can extract action items to project tools. The meeting ends, the work starts automatically. This is PLG acceleration waiting to be unlocked: every new workflow triggered by a Fireflies app is a retention hook and a shareability moment.

Positioning tension to resolve

Fireflies is too often described as a notetaker. It isn't one anymore.

Homepage hero copy still leans into note-taking language. The product has evolved well past that. The messaging hasn't kept pace. The risk: sophisticated buyers comparing tools see features, not a category. The opportunity: own "conversation intelligence" more explicitly, with the receipts to back it. Profitable, $1B, Fortune 500, real-time web search. Those facts tell a different story than "AI notetaker."

IT / Security Admin · Gate-keeper, not a user
"Before anyone installs this, I need to know where the recordings go, who can access them, and what happens if an employee leaves."
This persona never appears in Fireflies' user-facing copy, but they block or accelerate every enterprise deal. Their questions are specific: Is this SOC 2 Type II certified? Does HIPAA apply to our use case? Can we restrict recording to company-owned meetings only? What happens to data when an employee is off-boarded?

The missing asset: a one-page security brief written in the language IT admins speak, covering data residency, retention controls, SSO, access permissions, and audit logs. That document unlocks procurement faster than any feature comparison.

04 GTM Brief
Core GTM thesis

Fireflies' next growth motion: turn every AI app into a growth loop.

The 200+ AI apps suite is structurally underutilized as a distribution mechanism. Each app is a workflow change. Every workflow change is a sharing moment. When a sales manager sees a clean auto-generated call scorecard appear in their Slack after every call, they tell their peer at another company. That's referral growth baked into product behavior. The GTM brief below is built around activating this loop.

Segment 1: PLG expansion

Individual free users to team Business plans

The free user is typically an IC who adopted Fireflies without IT approval. The upgrade trigger: they use one AI app and realize the output is better when they can see teammates' meetings too. The GTM motion here is in-product: surface the team benefit at the exact moment a free user tries to reference a call they weren't on. The paywall should appear right at the "aha, I need my team on this" moment.

Segment 2: Gong/Chorus displacement

Mid-market sales teams, 10-100 reps

Companies with 10-100 sales reps paying $1,200+/user/year for Gong are exposed. A targeted campaign with a Fireflies cost model calculator could convert meaningfully. The message isn't "Fireflies is better than Gong," it's "Gong was built for 200-rep enterprise floors. If you have 25 reps, you're paying for infrastructure you'll never use." Displacement campaigns work when they're honest about the use case fit.

Segment 3: Global / distributed teams

60+ language support as the differentiator

No other meeting intelligence tool has cross-language support at this depth. A company with teams in India, Germany, Brazil, and the US has a real problem: their meetings happen in multiple languages and the institutional knowledge is siloed. Fireflies solves this out of the box. A vertical campaign targeting global-first companies (especially in tech and consulting) with this message is wide open.

Activation funnel: PLG loop

How a free individual user becomes a paid team.

01 Discovery Sees Fireflies in a meeting as a participant. Checks the feature. Signs up for free. Zero friction. Free
02 Activation First meeting summary lands in inbox. AskFred query retrieves a detail they needed from last week. Product value is immediate and personal. Free

04 Team need surfaces They want to search a colleague's meeting for context. They want shared smart filters across the team. They hit the team feature limit. Upgrade trigger
05 Champion converts They bring it to their manager. The manager upgrades for the team. Enterprise IT is looped in. One free user becomes 15 Business seats. Business

Every conversation your team has is a data point. A decision, a commitment, a customer signal, a competitive mention. Fireflies captures all of it, organizes it, and lets you ask it questions, trigger workflows from it, and search it six months later like it happened this morning.
Trusted by 20 million people across 500,000 organizations. Profitable since 2023. The AI teammate for teams who measure results, not just run meetings.
CTAs: "Start for free"  •  "See how teams use it"
Why this works: "Your meetings have been talking" is active and creates curiosity. It implies that something has been happening that the reader hasn't fully accessed yet. "Now they can actually think back" introduces the product benefit without naming the category. The body copy grounds it in concrete behavior: capture, organize, query, trigger, search. The trust signals (20M users, profitable) appear at the end as reinforcement, not the lead, which avoids the empty credential trap that "the #1 AI meeting assistant" framing falls into.

The first meeting assistant that knows what's happening in the room and outside it.
